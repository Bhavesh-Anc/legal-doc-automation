### 1. **Request for Order (FL-300)** ‚≠ê HIGHEST PRIORITY
**Why:** Most versatile and frequently used form in family law

- Enforce existing orders

**Fields Needed:**

- Case number

**Time to Build:** 2-3 hours

---

### 2. **Income and Expense Declaration (FL-150)** ‚≠ê VERY IMPORTANT
**Why:** Required for ALL support cases (child support, spousal support, attorney fees)
**Use Cases:**
- Child support calculations
- Spousal support determinations
- Attorney fee requests
- Modification of support

**Fields Needed:**
- Employment information (employer, income, hours)
- Income from all sources (wages, bonuses, rental, investments)
- Monthly expenses (housing, utilities, food, transportation, childcare)
- Assets (home value, cars, bank accounts, retirement)
- Debts (mortgage, credit cards, loans)
- Tax information

**Complexity:** HIGH (lots of financial fields)
**Time to Build:** 4-5 hours

---

### 3. **Responsive Declaration to Request for Order (FL-320)**
**Why:** Required to respond to FL-300
**Use Cases:**
- Respond to custody modification requests
- Oppose support changes
- Counter proposed orders

**Fields Needed:**
- Case information
- Which orders you agree/disagree with
- Facts supporting your response
- Your proposed orders
- Supporting evidence

**Complexity:** Medium
**Time to Build:** 2-3 hours

---

### 4. **Stipulation to Establish or Modify Child Support (FL-350)**
**Why:** When both parents agree on support amount
**Use Cases:**
- Agree on support without court hearing
- Modify existing support by agreement
- Faster than contested hearing

**Fields Needed:**
- Current support order (if modifying)
- Agreed new support amount
- Payment terms
- When it starts
- Both parties' signatures

**Complexity:** Low
**Time to Build:** 1-2 hours

---

### 5. **Marital Settlement Agreement (MSA)**
**Why:** Comprehensive agreement covering ALL divorce issues
**Use Cases:**
- Uncontested divorces
- Settle all issues at once (custody, property, support)
- Avoid trial

**Fields Needed:**
- Everything: custody, visitation, child support, spousal support, property division, debt division, retirement accounts, life insurance
- Most comprehensive form

**Complexity:** VERY HIGH
**Time to Build:** 6-8 hours

### 7. **Name Change After Divorce (FL-395)**
**Why:** Restore maiden name post-divorce
**Use Cases:**
- Change name back after divorce
- Required by some employers/banks

**Fields Needed:**
- Current name
- Name you want to restore to
- Marriage date
- Divorce case number
- Reason for name change

**Complexity:** Low
**Time to Build:** 1 hour

---

### 8. **Simplified Divorce (FL-800 Packet)**
**Why:** Faster divorce for simple cases
**Use Cases:**
- Married less than 5 years
- No children
- No real property
- Limited assets/debts
- Both parties agree

**Fields Needed:**
- Marriage date/location
- Separation date
- Property (under $47,000)
- Debt (under $6,000)
- No children checkbox
- Signatures

**Complexity:** Medium
**Time to Build:** 2-3 hours

---

### 9. **Paternity Petition (FL-200)**
**Why:** Establish legal fatherhood
**Use Cases:**
- Unmarried parents
- Establish custody/support when not married
- Get father's name on birth certificate

**Fields Needed:**
- Mother/Father/Child info
- Birth certificate details
- DNA test results (if any)
- Requested orders (custody, support)
- Father's acknowledgment or denial

**Complexity:** Medium
**Time to Build:** 2-3 hours

---

### 10. **Modification of Child Support (FL-390)**

- Current order details
- Current income (both parents)
- New circumstances
- Proposed new amount
- Guideline calculation

**Complexity:** Medium
**Time to Build:** 2-3 hours
